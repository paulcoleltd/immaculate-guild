1. Who We Are

Immaculate Guild Ltd ("we", "us", "our") is a professional cleaning company registered in England and Wales. We operate across Liverpool, Merseyside, and the wider North West. As a data controller, we are responsible for the personal information you share with us.

For any privacy matters, please contact us at:
Email: hello@immaculateguild.co.uk
Phone: 0151 234 5678

2. Data We Collect

We collect personal information when you contact us, request a quote, book a service, or visit our website. This may include:

  • Your name, email address and phone number
  • Your property address and details relevant to your cleaning requirements
  • Service preferences, access instructions and scheduling information
  • Payment details (processed securely; we do not store card numbers)
  • Technical data such as browser type, IP address, and pages visited (collected via cookies — see Section 8)
  • Communications you send us, including quotes requests and feedback

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiry and provide an accurate quote
  • To schedule, deliver and follow up on cleaning services
  • To process payment and manage your account
  • To send service reminders, booking confirmations and post-service follow-ups
  • To improve our website and the quality of our services
  • To meet our legal and regulatory obligations

We do not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling.

4. Lawful Basis for Processing

We process your personal data under the following lawful bases as defined by UK GDPR:

  • Contract: Processing necessary to deliver the services you have requested
  • Legitimate interests: Responding to enquiries, improving our services, and maintaining business records
  • Consent: Where you have opted in to receive marketing communications
  • Legal obligation: Where required by law (e.g. tax and accounting records)

5. Sharing Your Data

We do not sell, rent or trade your personal information. We may share your data with trusted third parties only where necessary to deliver our services, including:

  • Payment processors for secure transaction handling
  • Scheduling and CRM software providers
  • Email and communications platforms
  • Professional advisors (accountants, solicitors) bound by confidentiality

All third parties are required to handle your data securely and in accordance with UK GDPR. We do not transfer your data outside of the UK or EEA.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary:

  • Customer and booking records: 6 years (UK tax and accounting requirements)
  • Marketing contacts (with consent): until you unsubscribe or withdraw consent
  • Enquiries that did not result in a booking: 12 months
  • Website analytics data: aggregated and anonymised after 26 months

7. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Rectification: Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your data where there is no legal basis to retain it
  • Restriction: Ask us to pause processing of your data in certain circumstances
  • Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing
  • Withdraw consent: Where processing relies on consent, withdraw it at any time

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at hello@immaculateguild.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

8. Cookies

Our website uses cookies — small text files stored on your device — to help the site function correctly and to understand how visitors use it. We use:

  • Essential cookies: Required for the website to operate (no consent needed)
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand page visits and usage patterns (require consent)

You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings at any time. Disabling analytics cookies will not affect your ability to use the site.

9. Security

We take the security of your personal data seriously. We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect against unauthorised access, loss, or disclosure. These include encrypted communications (HTTPS), access controls, and regular security reviews.

No internet transmission is 100% secure. If you have concerns about the security of your data, please contact us immediately.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be published on this page with an updated revision date. Where changes are material, we will notify active customers directly. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
